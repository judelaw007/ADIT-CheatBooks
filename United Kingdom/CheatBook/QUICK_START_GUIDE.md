# ADIT UK CheatBook - Quick Start Guide

## Welcome!

This guide will help you navigate and use the CheatBook effectively for your ADIT UK exam preparation.

## First-Time Setup (Week 1)

### Day 1-2: Orientation
1. **Read the main README.md** in this folder - understand the philosophy
2. **Skim through 01_Battlefield_Intelligence/README.md** - know what intelligence is available
3. **Review 05_Appendices/** - familiarize yourself with quick reference materials

### Day 3-5: Strategic Planning
1. Study **01_Battlefield_Intelligence/Syllabus_Decoder** - prioritize topics
2. Review **01_Battlefield_Intelligence/Past_Questions_Analysis** - identify patterns
3. Read **03_Cross_Cutting_Skills/Time_Management** - plan your exam strategy

### Day 6-7: Template Introduction
1. Pick ONE high-frequency topic (e.g., Residence)
2. Read the TEMPLATE.md file for that topic
3. Review one worked example from past papers

## Main Study Phase (Week 2-8)

### Weekly Cycle (Repeat for each major topic)

**Monday-Tuesday: Template Learning**
- Study TEMPLATE.md for new topic
- Read through Key_Points.md
- Review legislation references

**Wednesday-Thursday: Application Practice**
- Work through Worked_Examples.md
- Attempt one example independently (untimed)
- Compare your answer to solution

**Friday: Timed Practice**
- Attempt one past paper question under timed conditions
- Use the template structure
- Review and identify gaps

**Saturday: Cross-Cutting Skills**
- Practice one skill from section 03
- Apply to multiple question types
- Drill calculations or time management

**Sunday: Review and Consolidation**
- Review week's templates
- Update personal notes
- Plan next week's topics

## Study Tools and Techniques

### The Template Card Method
1. Create flashcards with template structures
2. Practice recalling structure without notes
3. Time yourself - can you outline template in 2 minutes?

### The 3-Pass System for Past Papers
1. **First Pass**: Untimed, with notes
2. **Second Pass**: Timed, with templates only
3. **Third Pass**: Timed, exam conditions (no references)

### The Gap Analysis Log
Keep a running log:
- Topics you struggle with
- Common mistakes
- Time management issues
- Knowledge gaps

Review weekly and adjust study plan.

## Common Questions

**Q: Should I memorize every template word-for-word?**
A: No. Understand the structure and logic. Adapt to each scenario.

**Q: What if I run out of time on the exam?**
A: Review 04_Execution_Excellence/Exam_Day_Strategy for emergency protocols.

**Q: Which topics should I prioritize if short on time?**
A: Focus on Part A (50% of marks) - Residence, PE, Remittance Basis. Then CFCs and Transfer Pricing from Part B.

**Q: How many past papers should I do?**
A: Minimum 10 full papers. More is better, but quality > quantity.

**Q: Can I pass with just the templates?**
A: Templates are frameworks, not substitutes for knowledge. You need both.
