### Part A Question Characteristics

**Common Features:**
- **Fact-dense scenarios** (200-400 words of facts)
- **Multi-jurisdictional** (typically involving 2-3 countries)
- **Multi-part requirements** (usually 2-4 sub-questions)
- **Integrated topics** (e.g., residence + treaty + temporary non-residence)
- **Recent legislative changes** (examiners love testing new rules)

**What Examiners Reward** (from Examiner Psychology):
1. **Structured analysis** - Clear headings and logical flow (10-15% of marks)
2. **Application to facts** - Linking rules to specific scenario details (40-45% of marks)
3. **Correct legislation citations** - FA 2013 Sch 45, s.809VA ITA 2007, etc. (10-15% of marks)
4. **Balanced conclusions** - "On balance..." or "Insufficient facts to determine conclusively..." (10-15% of marks)
5. **Professional skepticism** - Identifying risks and alternative interpretations (5-10% of marks)

**What Examiners Penalize:**
- âŒ Knowledge dumping without application to facts
- âŒ Ignoring specific facts provided in the question
- âŒ Failing to answer all parts of multi-part questions
- âŒ Generic statements without technical depth
- âŒ Poor structure making marking difficult
