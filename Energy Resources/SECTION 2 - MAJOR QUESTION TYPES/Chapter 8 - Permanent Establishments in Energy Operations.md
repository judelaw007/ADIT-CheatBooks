### Why PE Matters in Oil & Gas

| Factor | Impact | Exam Relevance |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Project-Based Operations** | Drilling, construction, services are inherently temporary | Time threshold calculations critical |
| **Multi-Jurisdictional Teams** | Service companies move personnel across borders | Service PE day-counting complex |
| **Offshore Complexity** | Territorial waters, EEZ, continental shelf | Jurisdictional boundaries unclear |
| **High-Value Contracts** | Multi-million dollar service agreements | Profit attribution significant |
| **Subcontracting Chains** | Multiple entities on single project | Aggregation and anti-fragmentation rules |

### Article 5 OECD Model: The PE Definition

> **Article 5(1)**: "The term 'permanent establishment' means a fixed place of business through which the business of an enterprise is wholly or partly carried on."

| PE Type | Definition | Energy Sector Example |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Fixed Place PE** | Place at disposal, fixed geographically, degree of permanence | Field office, warehouse, workshop |
| **Construction PE** | Building site, construction or installation project >12 months | Pipeline construction, platform installation |
| **Service PE** | Services performed >183 days in 12-month period (UN Model) | Technical advisory, engineering services |
| **Dependent Agent PE** | Agent habitually concludes contracts on behalf of enterprise | Country manager signing contracts |
| **Offshore PE** | Drilling rigs, FPSOs in territorial waters meeting duration | Drilling rig >12 months (OECD) or >6 months (UN) |

### OECD vs UN Model: Critical Differences

| Aspect | OECD Model | UN Model | Exam Tip |
|--------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Construction PE** | 12 months | 6 months | Significant for developing countries |
| **Service PE** | Not in basic text | 183 days in 12-month period | UN Model more aggressive |
| **Attraction Principle** | Not included | Services performed "in connection with" PE taxable | Broader PE scope |
| **Insurance PE** | Agent must bind company | Collecting premiums sufficient | Rarely tested |
| **Offshore Rule** | Treaty dependent | Often 6-month threshold | Check bilateral treaty |

### BEPS Action 7: Anti-Avoidance Measures (Current as of 2025)

> **Key Development**: BEPS Action 7 significantly expanded PE definitions to prevent artificial avoidance, with many countries adopting changes via Multilateral Instrument (MLI).

| Anti-Avoidance Measure | Target Abuse | Application |
|----------------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Commissionaire Arrangements** | Agent technically "independent" but economically dependent | Look at substance, not legal form |
| **Contract Splitting** | Breaking contracts to stay under 12-month threshold | Aggregate connected projects |
| **Anti-Fragmentation** | Splitting activities to claim "preparatory/auxiliary" | Coherent business operation test |
| **Specific Activity Exemptions** | Warehousing, delivery, purchasing claimed as auxiliary | Only if genuinely preparatory/auxiliary |

**OECD 2023 Extractive Industries Proposal**: Discussion paper proposed **30-day threshold** for PE in extractive activities—monitor for treaty updates.

**Exam Calculation Example**:
```
Service company provides technical services under contract:
- Engineer A: 95 days in Country X
- Engineer B: 75 days in Country X
- Engineer C: 60 days in Country X

Treaty threshold: 183 days in 12-month period
```

## 8.5 Worked Examples

### Worked Example 8.1: Offshore Drilling Rig PE Analysis (Based on June 2025 Q8)

**Scenario**: DrillCo, a company resident in Country A, provides a drilling rig and crew to OilCo for exploration drilling in Country B's territorial waters. The contract is for 10 months, but delays extend operations to 14 months total. The Country A-Country B tax treaty follows the OECD Model with a 12-month construction PE threshold.

**Analysis Using Template 8B**:

```
CONSTRUCTION PE TIMELINE CALCULATOR
═══════════════════════════════════

Project: Exploration drilling for OilCo
Country: Country B (territorial waters)
Treaty: Country A - Country B (OECD Model basis)
Threshold: 12 months

TIMELINE ANALYSIS:
• Original contract: 10 months
• Actual duration: 14 months (including delays)
• Delays due to: Weather (2 weeks), equipment failure (6 weeks), permit delays (4 weeks)

KEY PRINCIPLE: Temporary interruptions do NOT stop the PE clock.
Duration runs from start of operations to completion.

NET DURATION: 14 months

AGGREGATION: Single project, no aggregation issues

CONCLUSION: 14 months > 12 months threshold
RESULT: CONSTRUCTION PE EXISTS
```

**Tax Consequences**:
1. DrillCo has a PE in Country B from month 12 onwards
2. However, most treaty positions apply PE status retroactively from day 1 once threshold exceeded
3. Article 7 profit attribution applies - profits attributable to drilling operations taxable in Country B
4. Country A must provide relief (credit or exemption) to avoid double taxation

**Mark-Scoring Points**:
- Identify PE type correctly (construction/installation PE under Article 5(3))
- Apply correct threshold (12 months OECD Model)
- Note that delays do not reset clock
- Discuss retroactive effect of exceeding threshold
- Apply profit attribution principles
- Mention double taxation relief

### Worked Example 8.2: Service Company PE Day-Counting (Based on December 2023 Q4)

**Scenario**: TechServices Ltd (resident in Country X) provides technical advisory services to multiple oil & gas clients in Country Y under separate contracts. Personnel presence in Year 1:

| Project | Client | Days |
|---------|--------|------|
| Project Alpha | OilCo A | 95 days |
| Project Beta | OilCo B | 65 days |
| Project Gamma | OilCo A | 45 days |

The Country X-Country Y treaty includes a UN Model-style service PE provision: 183 days in any 12-month period for the same or connected projects.

**Analysis Using Template 8C**:

```
SERVICE PE DAY-COUNT TRACKER
═══════════════════════════

SERVICE PE THRESHOLD: 183 days in 12-month period (UN Model)

PERSONNEL DAY COUNTING:
• Project Alpha (OilCo A): 95 days
• Project Beta (OilCo B): 65 days
• Project Gamma (OilCo A): 45 days

SAME OR CONNECTED PROJECT TEST:
• Projects Alpha and Gamma: Same client (OilCo A)
  - Are they connected? Consider: same field, related contracts,
    commercial interdependence
  - If connected: Aggregate = 95 + 45 = 140 days

• Project Beta: Different client (OilCo B)
  - Count separately: 65 days

SCENARIO A - If Alpha and Gamma are connected:
• OilCo A projects: 140 days - NO PE (< 183)
• OilCo B project: 65 days - NO PE (< 183)

SCENARIO B - If Alpha, Beta, and Gamma are all connected:
• This would be unusual unless OilCo A and B are related parties
• Total: 205 days - PE EXISTS (> 183)

MOST LIKELY ANSWER: No service PE, as projects for different
clients are typically not "connected"
```

**Mark-Scoring Points**:
- Identify service PE provision correctly
- Demonstrate day-counting methodology
- Apply "same or connected project" test
- Consider different scenarios
- Provide clear conclusion with reasoning

### Worked Example 8.3: Dependent Agent PE - BEPS Action 7 Application

**Scenario**: ForeignCo (resident in Country A) has a sales representative in Country B who identifies customers, negotiates contract terms, and sends agreements to head office for "approval" (which is routinely granted). No physical office exists in Country B.

**Pre-BEPS Analysis**:
Under original Article 5(5), agent PE requires agent "habitually exercising authority to conclude contracts in the name of the enterprise." Since contracts are signed by head office, arguable no agent PE.

**Post-BEPS Action 7 Analysis**:

```
DEPENDENT AGENT PE ASSESSMENT (Post-BEPS)
═════════════════════════════════════════

EXPANDED TEST (BEPS Action 7):
Agent PE exists if a person:
□ Habitually concludes contracts, OR
☑ Habitually plays the PRINCIPAL ROLE leading to conclusion of
  contracts that are routinely concluded without material modification

ANALYSIS:
1. Representative negotiates terms ──▶ Principal role in conclusion
2. Contracts routinely approved ──▶ No material modification
3. Legal formality of head office signature is disregarded

CONCLUSION: DEPENDENT AGENT PE EXISTS under BEPS Action 7

INDEPENDENCE TEST:
☐ Economically independent? NO - exclusive to ForeignCo
☐ Multiple principals? NO - works only for ForeignCo
☐ Business risk borne by agent? NO - acts on instruction

NOT an independent agent - cannot escape PE finding
```

**Mark-Scoring Points**:
- Distinguish pre and post-BEPS position
- Apply "principal role" test correctly
- Note that legal form (who signs) is not determinative
- Test for independence
- Reference MLI implementation status if relevant

## 8.6 Common Errors and Examiner Criticisms

### Error 1: Confusing PE Types

❌ **Wrong**: "The drilling rig creates a service PE after 183 days."

✅ **Correct**: "The drilling rig creates a construction/installation PE after 12 months (OECD) or 6 months (UN Model). Service PE rules apply to personnel providing services, not to equipment presence."

### Error 2: Failing to Check the Applicable Treaty

❌ **Wrong**: "The 12-month threshold applies" (without checking)

✅ **Correct**: "The applicable treaty between Country X and Country Y provides for a [6/12] month threshold for construction PE. This differs from the OECD Model because..."

### Error 3: Misapplying Day-Counting Rules

❌ **Wrong**: "Total days = Employee A (50) + Employee B (40) = 90 days"

✅ **Correct**: "For service PE, we aggregate personnel days for the same or connected project. Employees A and B both worked on Project X, so total days = 50 + 40 = 90 days against the 183-day threshold."

### Error 4: Ignoring Anti-Fragmentation Rules

❌ **Wrong**: "Each 10-month contract is separate, so no construction PE arises."

✅ **Correct**: "Where contracts are artificially split to avoid PE thresholds, BEPS Action 7 anti-fragmentation rules require aggregation. These three 10-month contracts for the same principal on adjacent sites should be treated as a single 30-month project, creating a PE."

### Error 5: Forgetting Profit Attribution

❌ **Incomplete Answer**: "PE exists, so Country B can tax."

✅ **Complete Answer**: "PE exists in Country B. Under Article 7, Country B may tax only the profits attributable to the PE. Using the Authorised OECD Approach, we hypothesise the PE as a separate enterprise and attribute profits based on functions performed, assets used, and risks assumed by the PE."

### Key Takeaways for Exam Success

1. **Always identify the PE type** - Fixed place, construction, service, or agent PE have different tests
2. **Check the treaty threshold** - OECD and UN Models differ; bilateral treaties may vary further
3. **Count correctly** - Construction PE counts calendar time; service PE counts personnel days
4. **Remember BEPS Action 7** - Anti-avoidance rules expand PE definitions significantly
5. **Complete the analysis** - Don't stop at "PE exists"; discuss profit attribution and tax consequences
6. **Consider mitigation** - Examiners reward practical structuring advice
7. **Reference specific articles** - Article 5(1), 5(3), 5(5), Article 7 - cite them explicitly
