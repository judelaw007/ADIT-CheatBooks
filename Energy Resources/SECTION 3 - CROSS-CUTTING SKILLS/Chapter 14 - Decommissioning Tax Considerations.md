## 14.1 TIMING OF DEDUCTION ISSUES

### Overview of Decommissioning Costs

**Definition**: Decommissioning involves the permanent closure and removal of oil and gas infrastructure at the end of productive life, including:

```
DECOMMISSIONING COST COMPONENTS
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. WELL ABANDONMENT                                              │
│    • Plugging and abandoning wells                               │
│    • Removal of wellhead equipment                               │
│    • Cement plugs and barriers                                   │
│                                                                  │
│ 2. PLATFORM/FACILITY REMOVAL                                     │
│    • Topside removal and disposal                                │
│    • Jacket/substructure removal                                 │
│    • Pipeline decommissioning                                    │
│                                                                  │
│ 3. SITE REMEDIATION                                              │
│    • Environmental clean-up                                      │
│    • Seabed clearance and debris removal                         │
│    • Monitoring and verification                                 │
│                                                                  │
│ UK INDUSTRY ESTIMATE: £41 billion total from 2023 onwards        │
│ 2024 ACTUAL SPEND: Record £2.4 billion                           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Tax Deduction Timing Options

**From December 2023 Q5**: "Oil and gas companies incur substantial costs when decommissioning assets... These costs may be deductible for tax purposes, either in the year they are incurred or over the useful life of the asset."

| Timing Approach | Description | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|-----------------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| **As incurred** | Deduct when costs paid | Immediate tax relief | Large deduction in single year |
| **Over useful life** | Spread deduction across asset life | Matches revenue timing | Delayed tax benefit |
| **Accelerated** | Front-loaded deductions | Early tax relief | May not match cash outflow |
| **Provisioning** | Deduct when provision made | Certainty of relief | May not align with actual costs |

### Accelerated Depreciation/Amortisation

**From December 2023 Q5**: "Some jurisdictions allow oil and gas companies to accelerate the depreciation or amortisation of decommissioning costs for tax purposes."

```
ACCELERATED vs STANDARD DEDUCTION TIMING
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Asset Life: 20 years | Decommissioning Cost: $100M              │
│                                                                  │
│ STANDARD APPROACH (Straight-line over asset life):              │
│ Year 1-20: $5M per year deduction                               │
│ Tax relief @ 30%: $1.5M per year                                │
│ NPV of relief @ 10%: $12.8M                                     │
│                                                                  │
│ ACCELERATED APPROACH (5-year write-off):                        │
│ Year 1-5: $20M per year deduction                               │
│ Tax relief @ 30%: $6M per year                                  │
│ NPV of relief @ 10%: $22.7M                                     │
│                                                                  │
│ BENEFIT OF ACCELERATION: $9.9M NPV advantage                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Asset Retirement Obligations (AROs)

**From December 2023 Q5**: "Asset retirement obligations (AROs), which oil and gas companies are required to recognise on their balance sheets for the estimated future costs of decommissioning assets."

```
ARO TAX TREATMENT FRAMEWORK
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ ACCOUNTING TREATMENT (IFRS/US GAAP):                            │
│ • Recognise liability at present value when obligation arises   │
│ • Capitalise as part of asset cost                              │
│ • Accrete liability over time (unwinding of discount)           │
│ • Depreciate capitalised amount over asset life                 │
│                                                                  │
│ TAX TREATMENT (Varies by jurisdiction):                         │
│                                                                  │
│ Option A: Follow Accounting                                      │
│ • Deduct depreciation of capitalised ARO                        │
│ • Deduct accretion expense                                      │
│ • Adjust for actual vs estimated costs at decommissioning       │
│                                                                  │
│ Option B: Cash Basis                                             │
│ • No deduction until costs actually incurred                    │
│ • Creates deferred tax asset on balance sheet                   │
│ • Full deduction when decommissioning occurs                    │
│                                                                  │
│ Option C: Modified Approach                                      │
│ • Deduct contributions to approved security fund                │
│ • Timing aligned with funding, not accounting                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 14.2 SECURITY AND TRUST ARRANGEMENTS

### Why Security is Required

Governments require financial security to ensure decommissioning obligations are met even if the licence holder becomes insolvent or transfers interests.

```
DECOMMISSIONING SECURITY MECHANISMS
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                  │
│ 1. DECOMMISSIONING SECURITY AGREEMENTS (DSAs)                   │
│    • Contractual commitments between licence holders            │
│    • Joint and several liability provisions                     │
│    • Parent company guarantees                                   │
│                                                                  │
│ 2. TRUST FUNDS                                                   │
│    • Ring-fenced funds held in trust                            │
│    • Contributions over field life                               │
│    • Released only for approved decommissioning                 │
│                                                                  │
│ 3. LETTERS OF CREDIT / BANK GUARANTEES                          │
│    • Third-party financial institution backing                  │
│    • On-demand security                                          │
│    • Costs of maintaining guarantee                              │
│                                                                  │
│ 4. INSURANCE PRODUCTS                                            │
│    • Decommissioning insurance policies                         │
│    • Transfer of risk to insurer                                 │
│    • Premium costs                                               │
│                                                                  │
│ 5. GOVERNMENT RELIEF DEEDS                                       │
│    • Contractual certainty on tax relief                        │
│    • UK Decommissioning Relief Deeds (DRDs)                     │
│    • Protects against future tax law changes                    │
│                                                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### UK Decommissioning Relief Deeds (DRDs)

**Current Data (2024-25)**:
- 109 DRDs entered since October 2013
- £14.7 billion in value unlocked
- Provides contractual certainty on tax relief

```
UK DRD MECHANISM
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PURPOSE:                                                         │
│ Government contractually guarantees future tax relief for        │
│ decommissioning costs, even if tax law changes                   │
│                                                                  │
│ HOW IT WORKS:                                                    │
│ 1. Company enters DRD with HM Treasury                          │
│ 2. DRD specifies "reference amount" (expected relief)           │
│ 3. If future tax changes reduce relief below reference amount:  │
│    → Government pays compensation to company                     │
│ 4. Creates certainty for investment and asset transfers         │
│                                                                  │
│ BENEFITS:                                                        │
│ • Facilitates asset sales (buyer certainty on relief)           │
│ • Reduces cost of capital                                        │
│ • Enables financing against future relief                        │
│ • Encourages late-life investment                                │
│                                                                  │
│ IMPORTANT (November 2025 legislation):                           │
│ • DRDs do NOT cover Energy Profits Levy (EPL)                   │
│ • EPL at 38% excluded from DRD compensation                     │
│ • Relief available: RFCT (30%) + SC (10%) = 40%                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Tax Treatment of Security Contributions

| Security Type | Tax Deductibility | Timing of Relief |
|---------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Trust fund contributions** | Usually deductible when contributed | As contributions made |
| **Letter of credit fees** | Deductible as financing cost | As incurred |
| **Insurance premiums** | Deductible as business expense | As paid |
| **Parent guarantee costs** | May be non-deductible (capital) | Depends on jurisdiction |
| **Actual decommissioning** | Deductible | When incurred |

## 14.3 SUCCESSOR LIABILITY PROVISIONS

### The Successor Liability Problem

```
SUCCESSOR LIABILITY CHAIN
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                  │
│ ORIGINAL LICENSEE (A)        YEAR 0: Licence granted            │
│         │                                                        │
│         ▼                                                        │
│ FIRST TRANSFEREE (B)         YEAR 10: A sells to B              │
│         │                                                        │
│         ▼                                                        │
│ SECOND TRANSFEREE (C)        YEAR 15: B sells to C              │
│         │                                                        │
│         ▼                                                        │
│ CURRENT HOLDER (D)           YEAR 20: C sells to D              │
│         │                                                        │
│         ▼                                                        │
│ DECOMMISSIONING              YEAR 25: Field exhausted           │
│                                                                  │
│ QUESTION: Who pays for decommissioning if D is insolvent?       │
│                                                                  │
│ SUCCESSOR LIABILITY ANSWER:                                      │
│ → Government can pursue C, B, and even A                        │
│ → All previous licensees potentially liable                     │
│ → "Joint and several" liability common                          │
│                                                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Tax Implications of Successor Liability

**From June 2025 Q1 Part 3**: Contracts may "request [the company] to leave all the production facilities and assets in place at the end of the agreement, while holding the company responsible for decommissioning the assets upon oil expiry."

```
SUCCESSOR LIABILITY TAX CONSIDERATIONS
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                  │
│ FOR THE SELLER (Previous Licensee):                              │
│ • Contingent liability remains post-sale                        │
│ • May need to maintain provisions on balance sheet              │
│ • Tax relief timing uncertain                                    │
│ • If called upon: deductible when payment made                  │
│ • Consider indemnity from buyer (taxable if received?)          │
│                                                                  │
│ FOR THE BUYER (Current Licensee):                                │
│ • Inherits full decommissioning obligation                      │
│ • Acquisition price should reflect liability                    │
│ • ARO recorded on acquisition                                    │
│ • Tax relief on own decommissioning costs                       │
│ • No relief if predecessor pays under successor liability       │
│                                                                  │
│ FOR INDEMNITIES:                                                 │
│ • Seller may indemnify buyer for excess costs                   │
│ • Buyer may indemnify seller against successor liability call   │
│ • Tax treatment of indemnity payments varies                    │
│ • Generally: payor deducts, recipient includes in income        │
│                                                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Tax Treaty Considerations

| Issue | Treaty Implication |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Deductibility of costs** | Usually domestic law, not treaty matter |
| **PE allocation** | Costs allocated to PE where incurred |
| **Withholding on payments** | Services may trigger withholding |
| **Parent funding** | Interest/equity characterisation |
| **Indemnity payments** | May be "other income" under Article 21 |

## 14.5 TAX INCENTIVES FOR DECOMMISSIONING

### Government Incentive Mechanisms

**From December 2023 Q5**: "Governments may even offer tax credits or incentives to encourage oil and gas companies to decommission assets in an environmentally responsible manner."

```
DECOMMISSIONING TAX INCENTIVES
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                  │
│ 1. TAX CREDITS                                                   │
│    • Direct offset against tax liability                        │
│    • May be refundable if no taxable income                     │
│    • Encourages timely decommissioning                          │
│                                                                  │
│ 2. LOSS CARRY-BACK                                               │
│    • Carry decommissioning losses to prior profitable years     │

│    • Immediate write-off of decommissioning costs               │
│    • No spreading over multiple years                           │
│                                                                  │
│ 4. TRANSFER OF TAX ATTRIBUTES                                    │
│    • Sell/transfer unused decommissioning credits               │
│    • Monetise losses if no income to offset                     │
│                                                                  │
│ 5. REDUCED RATES                                                 │
│    • Lower tax rate on decommissioning-related income           │
│    • Encourage responsible closure                               │
│                                                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Transfer of Unused Tax Credits

**From December 2023 Q5**: "In some jurisdictions, oil and gas companies may be able to transfer or sell unused tax credits associated with decommissioning activities."

```
TAX CREDIT TRANSFER MECHANISM
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                  │
│ SCENARIO: Company A has decommissioning losses but no income    │
│                                                                  │
│ WITHOUT TRANSFER:                                                │
│ • Losses carried forward indefinitely                           │
│ • No immediate cash benefit                                      │
│ • Value eroded by time value of money                           │
│ • Risk of permanent stranding                                    │

│ Decommissioning loss: $100M                                      │
│ Tax rate: 30%                                                    │
│ Tax credit value: $30M                                           │
│ Sale price (discounted): $25M                                    │
│ Company A receives: $25M cash now                                │
│ Company B saves: $30M in tax                                     │
│ Buyer benefit: $5M                                               │
│                                                                  │
```

```
TAX IMPLICATIONS OF DECOMMISSIONING

PART A: IMPLICATIONS FOR OIL & GAS COMPANIES (10 marks)

1. SUBSTANTIAL COST DEDUCTIONS
   • Well abandonment costs deductible
   • Platform/facility removal costs deductible
   • Site remediation expenses deductible
   • Timing: As incurred or over asset life (jurisdiction dependent)

2. ACCELERATED DEPRECIATION/AMORTISATION
   • Some jurisdictions allow accelerated write-off
   • Front-loads tax benefit
   • Improves NPV of decommissioning relief
   • Example: 5-year write-off vs 20-year asset life

3. TAX CREDITS AND INCENTIVES
   • Government incentives for responsible decommissioning
   • May be refundable if insufficient taxable income
   • Encourages timely and environmentally sound closure
   • Transfer of unused credits possible in some jurisdictions

4. ASSET RETIREMENT OBLIGATIONS (AROs)
   • Required recognition on balance sheet
   • Accounting vs tax treatment may differ
   • Creates deferred tax assets/liabilities
   • Adjustment when actual costs known

5. TAX PLANNING AND STRUCTURING
   • Optimize timing of decommissioning
   • Structure to maximize relief
   • Consider farm-out to parties with taxable income
   • Loss carry-back strategies

6. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
   • Documentation of decommissioning costs
   • Reporting to tax authorities
   • Maintain records supporting deductions
   • Coordinate with regulatory filings


PART B: IMPLICATIONS FOR GOVERNMENTS (5 marks)

1. REVENUE FOREGONE
   • Tax relief reduces government receipts
   • Cash refunds for loss carry-back
   • UK example: £1.45 billion annual relief

2. TIMING OF RELIEF
   • Front-loaded relief accelerates cash outflow
   • May occur when production (and revenue) declining
   • Need to budget for relief payments

3. POLICY DESIGN
   • Balance encouraging decommissioning vs revenue protection
   • Decommissioning Relief Deeds provide certainty
   • Consider environmental policy alignment

4. SUCCESSOR LIABILITY ENFORCEMENT
   • Ensures tax relief goes to party incurring costs
   • Prevents double relief (seller and buyer)
   • Requires robust tracking systems
```

## 14.9 ERROR PREVENTION

### Common Exam Mistakes

| Error | Why It's Wrong | Correct Approach |
|-------|---------------|------------------|
| Assuming immediate deductibility | Timing varies by jurisdiction | Check specific rules for timing |
| Ignoring ARO accounting/tax differences | Creates deferred tax issues | Address book/tax differences |
| Forgetting successor liability | Major risk in acquisitions | Always consider chain of title |
| Missing loss carry-back opportunity | Leaves value on table | Review carry-back provisions |
| Overlooking cross-border issues | Transfer pricing/WHT risks | Address intercompany aspects |
| Ignoring net zero implications | Increasingly examined | Consider early retirement scenario |
| Forgetting security tax treatment | Contributions may be deductible | Review security arrangement taxes |
